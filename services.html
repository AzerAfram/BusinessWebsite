Shoud you replace your Air Conditioner?


Replacing your air conditioning unit may seem unreasonable or even unprofitable at times, though replacement is highly recommended and necessary if:


  • Your air conditioning unit is 15 years or older. Old or overused units will increase electricity usage, breakdowns and cost of repairs/maintenance, poor air quality, uneven cooling, and pollution.


  • Your air conditioner breaks down or malfunctions frequently/yearly. Recurring breakdowns costs will add up and have the potential to wear down the unit and its warranty.


  • Your air conditioner can only run on R-22 freon. As of January 1, 2020, production and importation of R-22 freon is illegal in the U.S due to its highly negative impact on the environment. It is now extremely expensive due to scarce R-22 stocks nationwide. R-22-reliant units should be replaced when freon is diminished.



In the event of the above conditions, contact us to make sure that your future air conditioning unit will be compatible with your home size, needs, budget, and energy standards. Then we will schedule a date in which we will arrive with an energy-efficient air conditioning unit that complies with the Energy Star Standards as well as the right tools that will enable us to correctly remove the old unit and smoothly install your new air conditioner. Though if you no longer require your old unit, we will quickly load it up and transfer it out.

Do you need a repair?


Your air conditioner is not working properly and requires immediate attention if:


  • Your experiencing decreased airflow or inefficient cooling This can be the cause of a number of problems, to list a few: the AC is low on coolant, a leak anywhere in the system, and/or faulty air condenser.


  • The air conditioner produces unpleasant or unusual odors or noises inside or outside your home. Loud operation may be caused by issues in the air conditioner fan belt. Odors can vary in smell and so do the causes of these smells.


  • Your thermostat is delayed or incorrect. The issue here, at times, is with the thermostat, though it can also be the wiring inbetween the thermostat and the air conditioner, otherwise, the air conditioner itself is at fault.

When you experience any of the above, immediately power off the air conditioning unit in order to avoid elevating the problem and cause any more damage. The issue may be simple, though, so thourougly research the problem first.
